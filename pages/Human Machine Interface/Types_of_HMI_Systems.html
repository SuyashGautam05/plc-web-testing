Types of HMI Systems

HMI systems are classified based on their design, complexity, and application requirements. Different types of HMIs are used depending on the scale of automation and user interaction needs.

Basic or Text-Based HMI

Basic HMIs display information mainly in text form with limited graphics. They are used for simple machines and basic automation tasks.

Characteristics:

  • Low cost.
  • Limited display capability.
  • Suitable for small systems.

Graphical HMI

Graphical HMIs use symbols, icons, and mimic diagrams to represent machines and processes visually. These are widely used in industrial automation.

Features include:

  • Touchscreen operation.
  • User-friendly graphical interface.
  • Support for alarms and trends.

Panel-Based HMI

Panel-based HMIs are standalone units mounted on control panels. They integrate display, processor, and input devices in one enclosure.

Applications include:

  • Machine-level control.
  • Packaging and material handling systems.

PC-Based HMI

PC-based HMIs run on industrial computers. They offer high processing power, large displays, and advanced visualization features.

Advantages:

  • High flexibility and scalability.
  • Integration with SCADA and databases.
  • Suitable for large automation systems.

Web-Based HMI

Web-based HMIs allow remote monitoring and control through browsers using internet or intranet connections.

They enable:

  • Remote access and diagnostics.
  • Industry 4.0 and Industry 5.0 integration.
