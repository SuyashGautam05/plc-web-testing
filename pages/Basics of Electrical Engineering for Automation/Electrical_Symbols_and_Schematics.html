Electrical Symbols

Electrical symbols are standardized graphical representations used to denote electrical components in diagrams and drawings. They simplify the understanding of complex electrical circuits by providing a universal visual language. Common symbols represent components such as resistors, capacitors, switches, relays, motors, transformers, circuit breakers, fuses, sensors, and grounding points. Standardized symbols help engineers, technicians, and electricians correctly interpret circuit designs and ensure consistency across documentation.

Electrical Schematics

Electrical schematics are detailed drawings that show how electrical components are connected and how a circuit operates. They illustrate the flow of current, control logic, and power distribution within a system. In industrial automation, schematics are used for designing, installing, troubleshooting, and maintaining control panels and machinery. Clear and accurate schematics improve safety, reduce wiring errors, simplify maintenance, and ensure compliance with electrical standards.
Together, electrical symbols and schematics form the foundation for effective communication, design accuracy, and safe operation in electrical and industrial automation systems.

Types of Electrical Symbols

There are many electrical and circuit symbols that are used internationally around the globe. While they are most of the time standardized, which means that the same electronic symbol is used in different countries but we can still find some international differences in those symbols.

Wire Symbol

A wire symbol is a symbol which denotes a wire present in the electric circuit. There are mainly three types of wire symbols, which are mentioned below.

  • Electrical Wire: An electrical wire is a single conducting copper wire which is generally a cylindrical, flexible rod or strand of metal through which electric current flows.
  • Connected Wire: These are the wires which stay connected in an electric circuit and through which current flows easily. As they work as a medium for the flow of current from one point to another.

Ground Symbol

It can be defined as a reference point in a circuit which is known as the ground and has a voltage of 0 V. There are three types of ground symbols, which are mentioned below:

  • Earth Ground: This symbol depicts a point in an electric circuit that shows the measurement of voltage, a common returning path for electric current or grounding which is a direct connection of the electric circuit to the earth.
  • Common Ground: This symbol depicts the source voltage of digital logic ICs, which means that no analog signals are bonded into this reference plane.
  • Chassis Ground: It is the metal housing in which some electrical devices are enclosed. The chassis is unusually connected to the green ground wire of power lines present in our homes.

Logic Gate Symbol

There are three types of Logic Gate symbols, which are described as well as mentioned below.

  • AND or NAND Gate: AND gate gives the value of one only if all the operands are one, and otherwise, it has a value of zero. Whereas, the NAND gate gives the value of zero only if all the operands have a value of one, and otherwise, it has a value of one.
  • OR or NOR Gate: OR gate gives the value of one if at least one operand has the value of one, otherwise, it gives the value of zero. Whereas, the NOR gate gives the value of one if all operands have a value of zero and otherwise it has a value of zero.
  • XOR or NOT Gate: XOR gate is the digital logic gate that gives a true output when the number of true inputs put in it is odd. Whereas, a NOT gate is a logic gate that carries out logical negation.
  • XNOR Gate: It is an exclusive NOR gate which is made up by combining the exclusive NOT gate with the exclusive OR gate.
