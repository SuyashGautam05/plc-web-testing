VFD Parameters and Settings

VFD parameters and settings define how the drive operates and interacts with the motor and load. Proper configuration of these parameters is essential for safe, efficient, and reliable motor operation. Parameters are usually set through the VFD keypad or software interface.

Important VFD parameters include:

  • Motor rated voltage, current, and frequency.
  • Acceleration and deceleration time.
  • Minimum and maximum speed limits.
  • Control mode selection (V/f or vector control).

Motor Nameplate Parameters

Motor nameplate parameters must be correctly entered into the VFD to ensure proper operation. These include rated voltage, current, power, frequency, and speed. Accurate settings prevent overheating and improve motor efficiency.

Acceleration and Deceleration Settings

Acceleration and deceleration times control how quickly the motor speeds up or slows down. Proper settings reduce mechanical stress and prevent overcurrent or voltage faults.

Protection and Safety Parameters

VFDs include protection parameters such as overcurrent, overvoltage, undervoltage, and thermal protection. These settings safeguard the motor and drive from damage during abnormal conditions.
