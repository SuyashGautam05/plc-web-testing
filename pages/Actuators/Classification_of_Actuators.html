Classification of Actuators

  1. Based on the Type of Motion: Actuators can produce linear or rotary motion.
    • Linear actuators: Produce straight-line motion.
      • Examples: Hydraulic cylinder, pneumatic cylinder, lead screw actuators.
    • Rotary actuators: Produce rotational or angular motion.
      • Examples: Electric motors, stepper motors, servo motors.
  2. Based on the Source of Energy: Actuators are classified according to the energy they use:
    1. Electrical Actuators: Use electricity to produce motion.
      • Examples: DC motors, AC motors, solenoids, stepper motors, servo motors.
    2. Hydraulic Actuators: Use pressurized liquid (usually oil) to produce motion.
      • Advantages: High force and torque, smooth motion.
      • Examples: Hydraulic cylinders, hydraulic motors.
    3. Pneumatic Actuators: Use compressed air to produce motion.
      • Advantages: Fast response, cleaner than hydraulics.
      • Examples: Pneumatic cylinders, pneumatic motors.
    4. Mechanical Actuators: Use mechanical means like gears, levers, cams, or belts.
      • Examples: Rack and pinion, screw jacks, cam mechanisms.
    5. Thermal/Shape Memory Actuators: Use changes in temperature or material properties.
      • Examples: Shape memory alloys (SMA), bimetallic strips.
  3. Based on Function: Actuators can also be classified by the type of work they do:
    • Linear motion actuators: move objects in a straight line.
    • Rotary motion actuators: rotate objects.
    • Oscillatory actuators: swing back and forth.
