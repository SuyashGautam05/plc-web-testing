DC Motors

DC motor works on "Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction" which states that a current-carrying conductor placed in a magnetic field will experience a force. Let us understand it.

The North-South magnetic poles of the DC motor create a magnetic field inside the motor. Due to the power supplied to the motor, certain direct current flows into the coil. This situation can be described as in Faraday's law. A current-carrying wire which behaves as a conductor is placed in the magnetic field of the motor poles.

Since the magnetic field above the conductor is different from the magnetic field below the conductor, a flux is developed across the conductor. The difference in flux density between the top and bottom of the conductor will generate a torque and cause the wire to rotate at a certain speed.

This is how the electrical energy provided to the motor is converted into mechanical energy by the rotation of the current-carrying conductor.

What is DC Motor?

DC Motor is an electrical machine which, when provided with direct current electrical energy, converts it into mechanical energy. It is based on electromagnetic induction, where a conductor carrying current (normally a coil of wire) placed in a magnetic field experiences force to rotate. This rotation is used to perform mechanical work.

Construction of DC Motor

DC motor has such basic components, as a stator (stationary part of the element producing magnetic field) and a rotor part that rotates carrying winding or coil. When a DC voltage is connected to the coil, current flows through it and generates an electromagnetic field. When the magnetic field of this rotor interacts with that produced by the stator, a torque is induced which causes this piece to start spinning.

DC Motor Parts

A DC motor has the following main parts:

  1. Field System (Stator): The stator is the stationary part of the DC motor. It produces the magnetic field.

    Parts of Stator:

    • Yoke: Forms the outer frame and provides a magnetic path between poles.
    • Pole Core: Made of laminated iron; carries magnetic flux.
    • Pole Body: Supports the pole core and helps spread magnetic flux.
    • Pole Shoe: Spreads the magnetic flux evenly and supports the field winding.
    • Field Winding: Insulated copper wire wound on pole core; produces magnetic field when DC current flows.
    • End Plates: Cover and support internal parts like armature, commutator, and brushes.
  2. Armature:
    • Rotating part of the motor.
    • Converts electrical energy into mechanical energy.
    • Made of laminated silicon steel (0.3-0.5 mm thick) with windings.
  3. Commutator:
    • Reverses current direction in the armature.
    • Helps produce continuous and steady torque.
  4. Brushes:
    • Made of carbon (graphite).
    • Supply current to the rotating armature winding.
