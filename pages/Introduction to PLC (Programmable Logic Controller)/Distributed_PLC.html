Distributed PLC

A Distributed PLC system is designed for large-scale industrial processes where input/output devices are spread across wide physical locations. Instead of bringing all field wiring to a single control panel, distributed PLC systems place remote I/O modules near the field devices and connect them through industrial communication networks.

This architecture significantly reduces wiring complexity, installation cost, and signal delays. Distributed PLCs improve system reliability and make maintenance easier, especially in large plants.

Key features of distributed PLC systems include

  • Remote I/O stations connected via industrial networks.
  • Reduced cable length and panel congestion.
  • Faster response time for field signals.
  • High flexibility and scalability.

Distributed PLCs are commonly used in

  • Power plants.
  • Oil and gas industries.
  • Water treatment and wastewater plants.
  • Large manufacturing and infrastructure projects.

These systems are ideal for complex processes that require high reliability and continuous operation.
