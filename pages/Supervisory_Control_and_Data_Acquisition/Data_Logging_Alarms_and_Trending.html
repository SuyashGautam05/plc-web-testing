Data Logging, Alarms, and Trending

Data logging, alarms, and trending are core features of SCADA systems that support monitoring, analysis, and decision-making in industrial automation.

Data Logging

Data logging involves continuous recording of process parameters over time. Logged data is stored in databases and used for analysis, reporting, and compliance.

Advantages of data logging include:

  • Performance analysis.
  • Fault investigation.
  • Predictive maintenance.
  • Regulatory compliance.

Alarm Management

Alarms notify operators when abnormal conditions occur. SCADA systems generate alarms based on predefined limits and display them with priority and timestamps

Effective alarm management provides:

  • Early fault detection.
  • Improved system safety.
  • Reduced downtime.

Trending

Trending displays process data graphically over time. Trends help operators visualize system behavior and identify abnormal patterns.

Benefits of trending include:

  • Process optimization.
  • Fault diagnosis.
  • Performance monitoring.
