Introduction to Power Supplies

A power supply is an electrical device that converts electrical energy from one form to another to meet the requirements of connected loads. In industrial automation systems, power supplies are primarily used to convert high-voltage AC power into low-voltage, regulated DC power required by control devices such as PLCs, sensors, relays, HMIs, and communication modules.

Reliable power supply operation is crucial because automation systems heavily depend on stable voltage levels. Even small voltage fluctuations can cause malfunction, data loss, or damage to sensitive electronic components.

Types of Power Supplies Used in Automation

Industrial automation commonly uses the following types of power supplies:

  • AC to DC Power Supplies: Convert mains AC voltage (230 V or 415 V) into DC voltages such as 24 V, 12 V, or 5 V.
  • Switched Mode Power Supplies (SMPS): Widely used due to their high efficiency, compact size, and ability to handle wide input voltage ranges.
  • Linear Power Supplies: Provide clean output but are less efficient and bulkier; used where low electrical noise is critical.

Among these, 24 V DC SMPS is the most commonly used power supply standard in industrial automation.

Importance of Proper Power Supply Selection

Incorrect power supply selection can result in system instability or frequent failures. Selection criteria include:

  • Output voltage and current rating.
  • Total load calculation with safety margin.
  • Input voltage compatibility.
  • Environmental conditions.
  • Compliance with industrial standards.

Proper selection ensures uninterrupted operation and protects sensitive automation equipment.
