What is a Reed Switch?

A reed switch is an electrically operated switch that works using a magnetic field. It contains two thin metal contacts (called reeds) sealed inside a glass tube.

  • Activated by a magnetic field.
  • Used in position sensing applications.

Examples: Door/window sensors, security alarms, proximity sensing, speed sensors.

How Does a Reed Switch Work?

Reed switches work by reliably switching billions of operations using a simple magnet interaction to open and close contacts. First, the basic operation of a reed switch happens when it is brought into proximity of a permanent magnet, and the individual reeds become magnetized with the attractive polarity of the magnet. Second, when the external magnetic field becomes strong enough, the magnetic force of attraction closes the blades of the reed switch. Third, the magnetic field produced by the reed blades dissipate when the magnetic field is withdrawn.
Most importantly, since any residual magnetism on the reed blades would affect the opening and closing behavior. Therefore, the reed blades are annealed and processed removing any magnetic retention.
